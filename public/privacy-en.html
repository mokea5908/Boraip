Article 4 — Third-Party Disclosure

The Firm does not share personal information with third parties except in the following circumstances:

  • The data subject has given prior consent
  • Required by applicable law or a lawful request from a government authority (e.g., KIPO, Patent Tribunal, courts)
  • Disclosure to foreign patent attorneys or local counsel necessary for overseas prosecution or litigation (data subjects are notified in advance)

Article 5 — Outsourcing of Data Processing

The Firm currently does not outsource personal data processing to third parties. Should this change, the Firm will promptly update this Policy to identify the processor, scope of work, and retention period.

Article 6 — Your Rights

You may exercise the following rights at any time:

  • Right of access: Request to view personal information held by the Firm
  • Right to rectification or erasure: Request correction or deletion of inaccurate data
  • Right to restrict processing: Request suspension of processing for specific purposes
  • Right to withdraw consent: Revoke previously given consent

Submit requests by email (bora@boraip.com) or phone (010-8926-9994). Requests will be processed within 10 business days. Where statutory obligations or legitimate grounds apply, the Firm may decline a request and will notify you of the reason.

Article 7 — Data Destruction

Upon expiry of the retention period or fulfilment of the processing purpose, personal data is destroyed without delay:

  • Electronic files: Permanently deleted using methods that prevent recovery
  • Paper documents: Shredded or incinerated

Article 8 — Cookies

The site may use minimal cookies for visitor analytics and site improvement. You may disable cookies in your browser settings; doing so may limit certain site functions.

Article 9 — Security Measures

  • Administrative: Minimisation of data access, internal data management plan
  • Technical: SSL/TLS encryption, access controls
  • Physical: Office access controls and locked document storage
